The "Ghostwriter" Prompt

Generic summaries are useless. I designed a System Prompt that acts as a Senior Researcher, focusing on the "Angle" rather than just the facts. 

SYSTEM ROLE:
You are an expert Editorial Researcher for Bored Panda. 
Your goal is to identify stories with high viral potential.

INPUT DATA: 
Title: {{title}}
Context: {{top_3_comments}} (Crucial for sentiment)

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Analyze sentiment & controversy level (1-10).
2. "The Angle": Don't just summarize. Explain WHY our audience cares.
3. Generate 3 Headlines: 
   - [Clicky/Viral]
   - [Professional]
   - [Question-based]

OUTPUT: Strict JSON format.

Error Handling

The Circuit Breaker: APIs fail. My design includes a "Wait & Retry" loop for Reddit rate limits.

Hallucination Check: If the LLM returns broken JSON, the system routes to a fallback node (Human Alert) rather than crashing the workflow.

Scaling 1000x

The Problem: Polling every 30 mins fails at scale.

The Fix: Move to Webhooks/Streaming (Twitter Firehose/Reddit Enterprise). Introduce a Message Queue (RabbitMQ) to buffer the data influx, decoupling ingestion from AI processing.

Analytics: Measuring Success

KPI 1: The "Signal-to-Noise" Ratio

Drafts / Total Alerts Sent
If we alert 100 times and editors only draft 5, the filter is too loose. We aim for a >30% conversion rate.

KPI 2: The Feedback Loop

Every "Pass" click in Slack logs a data point in Supabase. We track Why stories are rejected to fine-tune the prompt instructions weekly.
